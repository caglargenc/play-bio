Shroomcards

A toolkit for exploring roles and purposes in more-than-human design

Much of today’s design still puts human needs, goals, and perspectives at the center—often ignoring the many other beings we share the world with and our inter-dependence with them for thriving together. More-than-Human (MtH) design challenges this by asking us to see animals, plants, fungi, and even technologies not as resources, but as beings we are deeply connected with— beings that shape us.

The Shroom Cards is a set of design cards that aims to help us explore this shift by encouraging us to reflect on the MtH relationalities in design practice. By concretising a set of relationalities (e.g., role and purpose cards) and leaving space for articulation of other perspectives (e.g., through empty relationality cards), Shroom Cards present initial starting points to articulate, reflect, and act on different relationalities in MtH design in practice. It is developed by researchers affiliated with Gameful Futures Lab (Research Center of Gameful Realities, Tampere University, Finland), as part of ongoing research, focusing on understanding MtH design in practice.
